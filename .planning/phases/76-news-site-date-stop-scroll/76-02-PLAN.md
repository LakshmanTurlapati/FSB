Execute the SCROLL-10 edge case test using MCP manual tools following the scrollToYesterdaysArticles workflow from the news-feed.js site guide. Test steps:

1. Use MCP navigate tool to go to https://www.bbc.com/news (primary target -- news site with infinite scroll article feed). Record the URL used and response status.

2. Use read_page to verify the news page loaded. Check for:
   - Article feed visible with article cards/containers
   - Article headlines present (h2, h3 elements)
   - Article timestamps visible (time elements with datetime attributes)
   - Cookie consent banner or notification prompts
   Record: page URL, article count in initial view, timestamp format observed.

3. Dismiss any cookie consent banners or notification permission prompts if present. BBC typically shows a cookie consent banner on first visit -- click the appropriate dismiss/accept button.

4. Calculate yesterday's date: today is the current date, yesterday = today - 1 day, format as YYYY-MM-DD for comparison with datetime attributes.

5. INITIAL BATCH -- READ ARTICLE TIMESTAMPS:
   - Use read_page or get_dom_snapshot to capture all visible articles
   - For each article, extract: headline text, link href, publication timestamp
   - Parse timestamps: prefer time[datetime] ISO 8601 values, fallback to visible date text
   - Compare each article's date to yesterday's date
   - Record: number of articles in initial view, date range (newest to oldest), any from yesterday

6. SCROLL-COMPARE-STOP LOOP (if no yesterday articles in initial batch):
   - Scroll down 600-800px using scroll(direction="down", amount=700)
   - Wait 1500-2000ms for DOM to stabilize (infinite scroll content load)
   - Read newly loaded articles -- extract timestamps and compare to yesterday
   - Track scroll cycle number and cumulative article count
   - Continue until an article date matches yesterday or is OLDER than yesterday
   - STOP when articles older than yesterday appear (we have passed through yesterday's articles)
   - Record: scroll cycles performed, articles per batch, when yesterday articles first appeared

7. EXTRACT YESTERDAY ARTICLES:
   - From all accumulated articles, collect those with dates matching yesterday
   - Extract for each: headline text, article link href, publication timestamp
   - Record: count of yesterday articles found, their headlines

8. COMPLETENESS CHECK:
   - Verify that the transition from today to yesterday articles was captured
   - Check if there is a clear boundary between today and yesterday articles
   - Note any articles with missing or unparseable timestamps

9. IF BBC PAGE INACCESSIBLE OR NO TIMESTAMPS FOUND:
   - Try fallback: navigate to https://www.reuters.com/world or https://www.cnn.com
   - Repeat the timestamp detection and scroll-stop workflow
   - If fallback also fails: document what was found and what is missing

10. OUTCOME CLASSIFICATION:
    - PASS = navigated to news site AND detected article timestamps AND scrolled to yesterday's articles AND stopped correctly AND extracted yesterday article info
    - PARTIAL = accessed news page but could not complete the full scroll-stop cycle (timestamp parsing issues, selectors mismatch, WebSocket bridge disconnect blocked live execution), OR accessed page but timestamps only partially extractable via HTTP
    - FAIL = could not access news page at all or no articles found
    - SKIP-AUTH = news page or article feed requires authentication

Record every step outcome (tool used, target, result, notes) for the diagnostic report.
