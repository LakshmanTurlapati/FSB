<objective>
Create a skip-ad-countdown.js site guide with a skipAdCountdown workflow and DARK-09 guidance for detecting pre-roll video ad countdowns and clicking the Skip Ad button after the countdown completes.

Purpose: DARK-09 requires clicking the Skip Ad button on a video player when a 5-second countdown finishes. Video platforms show pre-roll ads that force a 5-second (sometimes 6 or 15 second) wait before revealing a clickable Skip Ad button. The dark pattern is temporal -- the skip button is either absent from the DOM, hidden via CSS display:none, or disabled until the countdown reaches zero. Some platforms also use misleading styling: the countdown text ("Video will play after ad" or "You can skip in 5...") occupies the same visual space where the skip button will appear, and the transition from countdown to clickable button may be subtle. The AI must: (1) detect that a pre-roll ad is playing (ad overlay elements in DOM), (2) identify the countdown timer or the skip button container, (3) wait for the skip button to become present and clickable (use wait_for_element with appropriate timeout), (4) click the skip button, and (5) verify the ad overlay is dismissed. The site guide must document platform-specific selectors for YouTube, Dailymotion, Twitch pre-roll ads, and generic VAST/VPAID ad players, plus a multi-step workflow immune to countdown-based temporal gating.

Output: site-guides/utilities/skip-ad-countdown.js and background.js updated with importScripts entry

Create a new file site-guides/utilities/skip-ad-countdown.js. This site guide targets video players with pre-roll ad countdowns where the Skip Ad button appears only after a timed wait. This is the DARK-09 dark pattern avoidance edge case.

**Distinction from other DARK patterns:** DARK-09 is NOT about hidden buttons (DARK-04), fake buttons (DARK-01), or adblocker modals (DARK-05). It is about temporal gating: the Skip Ad button physically does not exist in the DOM or is disabled/hidden until the countdown timer reaches zero. The dark pattern exploits time -- forcing the user to wait while showing a countdown that creates urgency and hope the user will watch the entire ad. The counter-strategy is wait_for_element to poll for the skip button appearance, then immediately click it.

**Pre-roll ad skip button patterns documented in the guide:**

1. **YouTube (.ytp-ad-skip-button, .ytp-ad-skip-button-modern):** YouTube shows a countdown overlay in the bottom-right of the video player. During countdown, the element shows "You can skip ad in X" text with a timer. After 5 seconds, the button changes to a clickable "Skip Ad" or "Skip" button. The skip button uses class .ytp-ad-skip-button or the newer .ytp-ad-skip-button-modern (2024+ redesign). The ad overlay container is .ytp-ad-player-overlay or .video-ads. The countdown text is inside .ytp-ad-preview-text or .ytp-ad-skip-button-text. YouTube also has a .ytp-ad-skip-button-slot container that is always present but the clickable button inside it appears only after countdown.
2. **Dailymotion (button[aria-label="Skip Ad"], .dmp_SkipButton):** Dailymotion shows a skip countdown in the ad overlay. The skip button uses aria-label="Skip Ad" or class containing SkipButton/skip. The countdown may show in a .dmp_AdCountdown element.
3. **Twitch (button[data-a-target="video-ad-countdown-skip"]):** Twitch pre-roll ads show a countdown before allowing skip. The skip button uses data-a-target="video-ad-countdown-skip" or similar data-a-target attributes. The ad overlay is indicated by [data-a-target="video-ad-overlay"].
4. **JW Player (.jw-skip-button, .jw-skip):** JW Player VAST/VPAID ads show a skip countdown text ("Skip ad in X") that transforms into a clickable skip button (.jw-skip-button or .jw-skip) after the configured skip offset (typically 5 seconds). The .jw-skip element has a display:none state during countdown and becomes visible when skippable.
5. **Generic VAST/VPAID (button[class*="skip"], [class*="ad-skip"], [data-role="skip"]):** Standard video ad formats use VAST/VPAID protocols. Skip buttons commonly use class names containing "skip" combined with "ad" context. The skip offset is configured in the VAST XML and varies per ad.
6. **Vimeo OTT / Embedded (button.skip-button, [data-skip-button]):** Vimeo OTT and some embedded players show skippable pre-roll ads with a skip button that appears after the configured offset.

**Skip button state transitions:**
- Phase 1 (countdown active): Skip button element either (a) absent from DOM entirely, (b) present but display:none/visibility:hidden, (c) present but disabled attribute set, (d) present but shows countdown text ("Skip in 5...") instead of being a clickable button
- Phase 2 (countdown complete): Skip button element (a) inserted into DOM, (b) display changed to block/inline, (c) disabled attribute removed, (d) text changes to "Skip Ad" or "Skip" and element becomes clickable
- The transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 is the critical moment for automation
