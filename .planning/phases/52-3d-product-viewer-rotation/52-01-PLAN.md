**Step 2: Document 3D viewer interaction patterns**
3D product viewers typically work as follows:
- Product page shows static images by default
- User clicks a "3D View" or "360" button to launch the 3D viewer
- 3D viewer renders in a WebGL canvas element (Three.js, model-viewer, or similar)
- Horizontal mouse drag on the canvas rotates the model around the Y-axis (turntable orbit)
- Dragging the full width of the canvas typically rotates ~360 degrees
- Dragging half the canvas width rotates ~180 degrees

For Nike specifically:
- Nike uses `<model-viewer>` web component or embedded 3D iframe viewers
- The `<model-viewer>` element creates a shadow DOM with a canvas inside
- Interaction is via mouse drag on the model-viewer or its internal canvas
- Nike pages also have image carousel thumbnails that may include a 3D icon

For Sketchfab fallback:
- Sketchfab embeds models in an iframe with a WebGL canvas
- The viewer responds to mouse drag for orbit rotation
- Canvas is inside `iframe.player` or similar container
